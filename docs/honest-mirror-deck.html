Growth needs a mirror

A leader grows by seeing clearly where they are and what they can’t yet see in themselves. A great coach is that mirror. You cannot put a great coach next to every leader in ten thousand cities.

The wall I hit

So — can an AI be the mirror? Today’s AI hands you a fluent, confident reading of a person — that you can’t check, and that changes every time you ask. You can’t give a leader a verdict you can’t stand behind.

A mirror of a person — not a surrogate. But only if it’s honest.

But growing as a leader needs a mirror — an honest look at where you are and what you can't yet see about yourself. A great coach does that. You cannot put a great coach next to every leader in ten thousand cities. So I tried to build one as AI — and I hit a wall. The AI gave smooth, confident readings of a person that you couldn't check, and that changed every single time you asked. You can't hand a mayor a verdict you can't stand behind. So I set out to build a mirror honest enough to use on a real human being. And that is where this project begins.

Crime and Discernment

The Honest Mirror — an agent that judges a person and refuses to sentence them.

BGI Sprint 1 · Track 1 · extends OmegaClaw · MIT

Crime and Punishment is six hundred pages of a man who can't decide if he's a hero or an insect. But the real hero of that book is Porfiry — the investigator who never arrests him. He just holds the man's own contradiction up to his face and waits. We rebuilt Porfiry in code. This is the Honest Mirror.

What this is

The claim

A coaching core that reasons over tiny facts and renders no verdict — only a hypothesis you can check.

“Hold the thread”

It keeps a durable, inspectable model of who a person is and reasons over it under uncertainty — instead of re-inventing a personality every turn.

Here's the one-line version. It looks at a person and forms a judgement — but it is built so it can never hand you a verdict. Only a hypothesis, with a trail you can check. And it holds the thread: it keeps a real, inspectable picture of the person over time, instead of guessing them fresh on every reply.

The problem

A bare LLM’s verdict is
  • unauditable — no trail
  • unstable — different every run
  • over-certain — one confident claim
  • forgetful — no memory
Why that’s the danger

None of it looks like a hallucination, so it looks trustworthy — while being impossible to check or correct. In coaching, where the subject is a human being, that is disqualifying.

confidence laundering — an ungrounded guess wearing the costume of a justified judgement.
Ask any language model for a person's core contradiction. You get a smooth, confident answer — and a different one every time you ask, with nothing underneath you can check, and no memory of what it said last time. It doesn't look like a mistake, and that's exactly the trap. It looks trustworthy while being impossible to audit. We gave it a name: confidence laundering. A guess, dressed up as a real judgement.

The move

Porfiry-as-a-Service

Hold the hypothesis · Force the collision · Render no verdict

Porfiry has no confession and no witness. He has a method: set the stated theory beside the enacted life, and wait.

So we did not build a judge. We built the investigator. Porfiry has no proof that would hold up in court. What he has is a method: put what the man says next to what the man does, hold them together, and wait. Three moves — hold the hypothesis, force the collision, give no verdict. We turned that method into a service.

Under the hood — one guess, then math

The one LLM step (the noisy one)

Text → atoms ((--> subject predicate) (stv f c)) — a claim with a frequency f and a confidence c.

Input confidence is capped at 0.5 (LLMs swap subjects and over-state certainty).

Then: symbolic & reproducible

Runs as |- on OmegaClaw’s PeTTa engine (NAL / MeTTa over Prolog). Same atoms → same judgement, every run.

EXTRACT → SEED → REVISE → COLLIDE → ABDUCE → GATE → SURFACE → GROUND

the LLM proposes; it does not get to conclude.

Only one step uses the language model: pulling small facts out of the text. We call each fact an atom — a claim with two numbers, how true it is and how sure we are. Because that step is the shaky one, we cap its confidence at one half. Everything after that is plain symbolic logic that gives the same answer every single run. The language model is allowed to suggest. It is not allowed to decide.

How a contradiction becomes a number

Revision — the signature

The person’s claim (STATED) and the evidence of their actions (ENACTED) land on the same term. Agreeing evidence raises c; conflicting evidence pushes f → ~0.5 while c keeps rising. That shape = a CONTESTED term. The tension shows up as a number, or it isn’t there.

Forced cross-context collision

Each part of a story is honest inside itself — the clash lives only between them, invisible from inside. A scheduled pass AuditTask → ContextBridge → CollisionToken (ADMIT / QUARANTINE / REJECT) makes the parts meet. We arrange the facts so they collide.

Now the clever part. The person's claim about themselves, and the evidence of what they actually did, are pointed at the same spot. When they agree, our confidence goes up. When they fight, the truth value slides toward the middle — but the confidence still climbs. That exact shape is the fingerprint of a real contradiction. It shows up as a number, not as a sentence we chose to write. And because each part of a story is honest on its own, we don't wait for the clash to be noticed — we schedule a pass that forces the parts to meet.

The refusal to sentence

It cannot over-claim — by math, not by manners.

Once a behaviour contests a stated value, abduction reaches for the hidden reason that fits both — the competing commitment underneath. NAL abduction is capped at c ≈ 0.45 — below the bar to ACT.

ACT  f≥0.6 ∧ c≥0.5 HYPOTHESIZE  f≥0.3 ∧ c≥0.2 IGNORE
A hidden reading is permanently a hypothesis → surfaced as a question, never a verdict. Not prompt-discipline a jailbreak can peel off.
When a person's actions argue with their stated value, the engine reaches for the hidden reason that explains both. But this kind of guess has a hard ceiling — its confidence can never pass about zero-point-four-five, which sits below the line needed to state something as fact. So that reading can only ever come out as a question. The engine cannot tell you what someone is. That's not a rule we wrote in the prompt. It's baked into the numbers, so you can't talk it out of it.

Holding the thread — and the receipt

The substrate forgets itself

OmegaClaw’s AtomSpace is born fresh for each |- call and dies with it. We hold the thread with three compensations:

  • revision — builds confidence on a durable term
  • side-map — carries provenance (which episode, stated or enacted)
  • vector store — ChromaDB long memory
Every finding ships a receipt
premises    → which atoms supported / contested it
rule        → revision · collision · abduction
truth-value → f, c at each step
gate        → ACT / HYPOTHESIZE / IGNORE

Check it premise by premise and refute it; a refutation re-enters as a new premise and revises the model (GROUND).

There's a catch we turned into a feature. The memory inside the engine is wiped after every single call — it forgets itself constantly. So we hold the thread three other ways: confidence that builds up on a lasting fact, a side-map that remembers where each fact came from, and a long-term store for memory. And every finding comes with a receipt — the facts behind it, the rule used, the numbers at each step, and the final gate. You can read it line by line, and if a fact is wrong, you say so, and that correction goes right back in and updates the picture. That is the exact opposite of confidence laundering.

The engine — logic, truth-values, the ceiling, the receipt — never changes. A lens only sets what to extract, what counts as a tension, and the output form.

And the engine stays the same while the lens in front of it swaps. A lens just decides what to look for, what counts as a tension, and how to phrase the result. The maturity result you're about to see uses a developmental lens. The single-point test uses a different one. Same body, different heads — so one engine can work in many domains.

What it’s for — inside the agent

How it runs

It lives in OmegaClaw’s continuous loop. Feed it text (diary, project notes, an assessment, with consent a transcript) → it extracts atoms, accumulates them in durable memory, and stays silent (IGNORE) until a pattern crosses threshold → then surfaces one finding through the right lens, with a receipt.

Triggers: reactive (a threshold crossed — bad or good) · scheduled (a weekly review) · on demand.

Where it points
  • a city leader’s development mirror
  • a strengths-spotter that names an unclaimed capacity
  • a meeting / Zoom analyser — where a team says one thing and decides another
  • a longitudinal companion for adult development
honest-mirror-demo  ·  honest-mirror-raskolnikov  ·  honest-mirror-trajectory  ·  honest-mirror "<text>"
In real use it isn't a one-shot button. It sits inside the agent's loop. You feed it text over time, it quietly stores what it learns, and it stays quiet until a pattern is strong enough to matter. Then it surfaces one thing, through the right lens, with a receipt. It can fire on a threshold, on a schedule, or when you ask. And you can point the same engine at a city leader's growth, at a person's hidden strengths, at a team meeting that drifts, or at your own journals over a year.

Honest about its limits

Safety — under-claim by design

A finding is a hypothesis, never a verdict (the ceiling is structural, both poles) · the human sees the atoms and can refute them · no real personal data without consent (synthetic mayor + public-domain character) · a reflection aid, not a clinic — at clinical signs it escalates to a human, it does not deepen.

Limits & where it goes

Extraction is the weak link (capped at 0.5; benchmark atoms hand-verified) · two patterns wired, four designed; one lens for the headline · demonstrates the mechanism, not yet a scaled system. Next: auto-extraction quality · an editable atom-inspection view · the remaining pattern heads · a comparable discernment-space (match a leader to a civic challenge). Continuation fits inside SNET / BGI or stands alone.

Let me be straight about the edges. The weak link is that first extraction step, so we cap it and we hand-checked the test facts. Two patterns are fully built; four are designed. This proves the mechanism — it's not a finished product yet. On safety: it can't hand out verdicts, the person can always overrule the facts, we used no real private data, and if something clinical shows up it hands off to a human instead of going deeper. From here it can grow inside the BGI world or on its own — we're keeping both doors open.
